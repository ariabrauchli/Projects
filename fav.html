Cat Repellant 2000

In Object, I tackled a pet peeve by creating an Arduino project aimed at resolving my cat Tai's study-time interruptions.

The technical journey involved programming a Servo, designing a custom wooden box with Makercase and Rhino, and overcoming setbacks like measurement oversights and compatibility issues. Despite challenges, the assembly resulted in a whimsically spinning toy.

Testing the device on Tai during a demo revealed initial disinterest, earning it the nickname "Cat Repellent 2000." Encouraged by feedback, I further enhanced the project by adding a motion sensor and a chittering noise to mimic prey, overcoming challenges like finding suitable audio online and resolving compatibility issues.

The refined design, complete with engraved details and a breadboard, was put to the test with a pink feather swap. The heartwarming result: walking into my living room one afternoon to find Tai playing independently, confirming the project as a delightful and successful solution to my study interruptions.
